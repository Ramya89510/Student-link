Chart Types Recap!

Column Chart

Uses vertical bars to compare values across categories. Taller bars mean larger values. Great for showing changes over time or comparisons between different items.

Bar Chart

Similar to column charts, but uses horizontal bars. Useful when category names are long or there are many categories to compare.

Pie Chart

Shows parts of a whole as slices of a pie. Each slice represents a percentage of the total. Best for showing proportions, but only for a few categories.

Line Chart

Connects data points with lines to show trends or changes over time. Excellent for tracking continuous data like temperature or stock prices.

Understanding Chart Elements

  • Chart Title: Tells you what the entire chart is about. It's usually at the top.
  • X-axis (Horizontal Axis): Represents categories or time periods. It's usually at the bottom.
  • Y-axis (Vertical Axis): Represents the values or measurements. It's usually on the left side.
  • Data Series/Bars/Lines/Slices: These are the actual visual representations of your data (e.g., the bars in a bar chart, the line in a line chart, the slices in a pie chart).
  • Legend: Explains what different colors, patterns, or symbols in the chart represent, especially when there are multiple data series.
